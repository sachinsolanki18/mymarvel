Hulk, the alter ego of scientist Bruce Banner, transforms into a massive green-skinned giant when angered. Powered by gamma radiation, Hulk possesses immense strength, durability, and a near-limitless capacity for destruction. Despite his rage-driven nature, he often fights to protect humanity, battling powerful foes and inner struggles, making him one of Marvel’s most complex and iconic heroes.

Iron Man, or Tony Stark, is a billionaire inventor and genius engineer who builds a powerful suit of armor to save his life and become a hero. Equipped with advanced technology, flight, and a wide range of weapons, he protects the world from powerful threats. Iron Man’s wit, intellect, and sacrifice make him a symbol of modern heroism.

Spider-Man, also known as Peter Parker, gains spider-like abilities after a radioactive spider bite. With enhanced agility, wall-crawling skills, and web-shooters, he fights crime in New York City. Guided by the principle “with great power comes great responsibility,” Spider-Man balances a normal life with superhero duties, making him one of Marvel’s most relatable and beloved characters.

Black Panther, also known as T’Challa, is the king of Wakanda — a technologically advanced and secretive African nation. With enhanced strength, agility, and senses granted by the Heart-Shaped Herb, he dons a vibranium suit to defend his people. A brilliant strategist and noble leader, Black Panther stands as a symbol of honor, tradition, and power in the Marvel universe.
